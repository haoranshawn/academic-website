Service

Service Summary

During my time at Bradley University, I have demonstrated a strong commitment to service at the university, college, department, professional, and community levels. Internally, I have contributed to governance and curriculum development through my roles as a University Senate Senator and a member of the Core Curriculum Committee. At college level, I have helped advance diversity, equity, and inclusion as part of the DEI Task Force, and have supported faculty development by serving on the R&T Committee and participating in a range of professional development activities. Within my department, I have coordinated MIS programs, MIS search committee, and actively participated in student recruitment and retention initiatives, including the New Student Welcome.

Beyond campus, I have made important contributions to my field by reviewing papers for leading academic associations such as the International Conference on Information Systems (ICIS) and The Midwest United States Association for Information Systems (MWAIS), where I also served as a session chair. My consulting work with a robotics startup further illustrates my dedication to linking academic research and industry practice.

A vital aspect of my service has been to connect classroom learning with real-world experiences. I regularly invite industry guest speakers to engage students with emerging trends, and I have coordinated a series of consulting projects with the City of Peoria that give students hands on opportunities to apply what they learn. These projects emphasize societal impact by addressing issues such as blight reduction, economic disparities in the local community, digital inclusion, and support for minority and women owned businesses.

My broader societal impact is evident in my role as a reviewer and contributor for the Joint United Nations Programme on HIV/AIDS, as well as my volunteer work at a food bank and an animal shelter. I have also strengthened ties between the university and the surrounding community by organizing the Social Impact Challenge and coordinating Business Analytics Day, which brings together academic and industry partners.

These service contributions underscore my dedication to institutional governance, student success, professional engagement, and community involvement, all of which align with the mission of Bradley University and the broader field of information systems.
