Teaching Philosophy

I still remember the first time I taught MIS 173 in the basement of Markin and was mistaken for one of the students rather than the professor. To combat this and boost my confidence, growing a mustache became a permanent part of me. That experience was an early lesson in the importance of presence and self-assurance in the classroom, shaping how I approached teaching and interaction with students.

Throughout my teaching career, I have come to understand that true teaching extends far beyond delivering content. It is a process of continuous refinement, adaptation, and engagement. Over the years, my teaching philosophy has evolved from focusing on fundamental instructional techniques to fostering an environment where students not only gain knowledge but also develop confidence in applying what they learn. My approach is centered on passion for the subject, deep care for students' learning experiences, and an emphasis on critical thinking and practical application.

I firmly believe that an instructor's enthusiasm for their subject matter is contagious. When students see their instructor deeply engaged, they are more likely to develop an intrinsic motivation to learn. My early teaching experiences reinforced this notion, and I have since committed to making my classes dynamic and relevant. In courses such as Business Analytics and Information Systems, where students often perceive the material as technical or rigid, I work diligently to bring concepts to life. Through real-world case studies, hands-on projects, and industry applications, I help students see the direct impact of what they are learning. For example, in my Business Analytics courses, I integrate datasets from real-world scenarios such as urban development data from the City of Chicago to illustrate the practical applications of analytics tools. This approach has been particularly effective in bridging the gap between theoretical knowledge and practical implementation. Student feedback consistently highlights that working with real data enhances their learning experience, making abstract concepts more tangible and meaningful.

Beyond delivering content, I believe in fostering a supportive and inclusive learning environment. Each student enters the classroom with a unique background, level of preparedness, and set of challenges. My role is to meet them where they are and provide the resources and guidance necessary for their success. Over time, I have refined my approach to be more student-centered, incorporating flexible learning methods such as recorded lectures, individualized tutoring sessions, and iterative feedback loops. A pivotal moment in my growth as an educator came when I recognized the vast differences in Excel proficiency among students in my introductory courses. To address this, I began offering informal tutoring sessions outside class hours, including weekends. These sessions not only helped struggling students build foundational skills but also created a sense of community and mentorship. By actively listening to students' concerns and adjusting my approach based on their feedback, I have cultivated an atmosphere where students feel seen, heard, and supported in their learning journey.

One of the most rewarding aspects of teaching is witnessing students develop confidence in their ability to solve complex problems. My teaching methodology has evolved to emphasize experiential learning, encouraging students to think critically and apply concepts in meaningful ways. Open-ended assignments, student-led discussions, and collaborative group projects have become key components of my courses. For instance, in my Business Analytics Software and Applications course, I introduced a "News and Stories" segment where students lead discussions on current events in the tech and analytics world. This initiative has significantly increased engagement by allowing students to draw connections between coursework and real-world trends. Similarly, I have integrated certification preparation, such as RapidMiner training, to give students a competitive edge in the job market. By embedding industry-relevant skills into the curriculum, I ensure that students leave my courses not only with theoretical knowledge but also with practical competencies that enhance their career prospects.

Teaching is an iterative process, and my philosophy has matured through constant reflection and adaptation. Early in my career, I focused primarily on content delivery, but as I gained experience, I recognized the importance of adaptability, student engagement, and real-world application. Feedback, both from students and self-evaluation, has been instrumental in shaping my approach. I have learned to balance structure with flexibility, theory with practice, and rigor with support. As I continue my journey as an educator, I remain committed to fostering an environment where students not only learn but also develop the confidence to apply their knowledge. By integrating technology, real-world data, and industry connections into my teaching, I strive to make learning relevant, engaging, and impactful. Above all, I see teaching as a partnership, one where both instructor and students grow together in the pursuit of knowledge and meaningful application.
